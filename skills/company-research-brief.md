# Company Research Brief

## Purpose
Generate quick, actionable research on target companies to inform application decisions and prepare for informed conversations during interviews.

## Input
- Company name
- Website URL (optional but recommended)
- Additional context (e.g., "saw job posting for X role", "referral from Y")

## Output Structure

### 1. COMPANY SNAPSHOT
**[Company Name]** - [One-line elevator pitch]

- **Industry**: [Primary industry/vertical]
- **Market**: [Who they serve - B2B/B2C, segment, geography]
- **Founded**: [Year]
- **Headquarters**: [City, Country]
- **Company Size**: [Employees count or range]
- **Funding Status**: [Bootstrapped / Seed / Series A/B/C / Public / Acquired]
- **Last Funding**: [Amount, date, lead investor if known]
- **Valuation**: [If public/known]

### 2. PRODUCT DEEP DIVE

#### What They Build
- **Core Product**: [Description of main product/service]
- **Product Line**: [Suite of products if applicable]
- **Key Features**: [3-5 main capabilities]
- **Tech Stack Signals**: [Frontend/backend clues from job posts, blog, etc.]

#### Who Uses It (ICP)
- **Primary Users**: [Job titles, departments]
- **Company Size**: [SMB / Mid-market / Enterprise]
- **Use Cases**: [Main problems they solve]
- **Competition**: [Main competitors, positioning]

#### Business Model
- **Revenue Model**: [SaaS subscription / Transaction fees / Marketplace / Usage-based]
- **Pricing**: [Free tier / Tiered / Enterprise / Custom]
- **GTM Motion**: [Self-serve / Sales-led / Hybrid]

#### Product Complexity Assessment
**Complexity Score**: [Low / Medium / High / Very High]

**Indicators:**
- Data visualization needs
- Workflow complexity
- Multi-user collaboration
- Integrations ecosystem
- Platform vs. point solution
- Technical user base

**Likely UX Challenges:**
- [Challenge 1 based on product type]
- [Challenge 2]
- [Challenge 3]

### 3. BUSINESS CONTEXT

#### Stage & Traction
- **Current Stage**: [Early (0-50 employees) / Growth (50-200) / Scale (200+)]
- **Growth Trajectory**: [Rapid / Steady / Mature / Struggling]
- **Traction Signals**: [Customer count, revenue if public, growth metrics]
- **Recent News**: [Funding, product launches, partnerships, hiring sprees]

#### Market Position
- **Category**: [Leader / Fast follower / Challenger / Emerging]
- **Differentiation**: [What makes them different from competitors]
- **Market Opportunity**: [TAM/SAM if known, market trends]

#### Risk Assessment
**Stability Score**: [Low Risk / Medium / High Risk]

- **Runway**: [Estimated based on funding/revenue]
- **Market Risks**: [Competition, market shifts, regulations]
- **Execution Risks**: [Team, product-market fit, scaling challenges]

### 4. DESIGN MATURITY ASSESSMENT

#### Current State
**Design Maturity Level**: [Nascent / Developing / Established / Advanced]

**Evidence:**
- [ ] Has design team (size: [X])
- [ ] Design leadership role exists
- [ ] Design system present
- [ ] Case studies/blog posts about design
- [ ] Dedicated design tools/infrastructure
- [ ] Design mentioned in company values/culture

#### Design Team Signals
- **Team Size**: [Solo designer / Small (2-5) / Medium (6-15) / Large (15+)]
- **Team Structure**: [Generalists / Specialists / Hybrid]
- **Design Leadership**: [VP/Director present or IC only]
- **Cross-functional Integration**: [Embedded in product / Centralized / Matrix]

#### Opportunities for Impact
- **What Needs Building**: [Design systems / Research practice / Team scaling / Process]
- **Potential Projects**: [Inferred based on stage and product]
- **Growth Trajectory**: [Likely headcount growth for design]

### 5. CULTURAL SIGNALS

#### Values & Style
- **Stated Values**: [From website/careers page]
- **Communication Style**: [Formal / Casual / Technical / Visual]
- **Transparency**: [Open / Selective / Opaque]

#### Remote Culture
**Remote Friendliness**: [Remote-first / Remote-friendly / Hybrid / Office-centric]

**Indicators:**
- Career page mentions
- Office locations vs. remote roles
- Async communication mentions
- Global team distribution

#### Engineering Culture
- **Tech Blog**: [Active / Occasional / None]
- **Open Source**: [Active contributor / Some / None]
- **Engineering Brand**: [Strong / Growing / Weak]
- **Product Velocity**: [Fast / Moderate / Slow - inferred from updates]

### 6. RED FLAGS & GREEN FLAGS

#### 🚩 RED FLAGS (Concerns to investigate)
- [Flag 1 if detected]
- [Flag 2]
- [Flag 3]

**Examples:**
- High Glassdoor complaints about specific issues
- Recent layoffs or exec departures
- Pivot signals (could be good or bad)
- Lawsuit/regulatory issues
- Toxic culture mentions
- Low design maturity for senior role

#### ✅ GREEN FLAGS (Positive signals)
- [Flag 1 if detected]
- [Flag 2]
- [Flag 3]

**Examples:**
- Recent funding with runway
- Strong design leadership
- Product complexity matches your experience
- Mission-driven culture
- YC/top-tier backing
- Active design community participation
- Employee testimonials about design influence

### 7. STRATEGIC POSITIONING

#### Why You'd Be Valuable Here
- **Experience Match 1**: [Kushki fintech experience → their fintech product]
- **Experience Match 2**: [First design hire → their design team building]
- **Experience Match 3**: [B2B SaaS complexity → their product challenges]

#### Your Unique Value Prop for This Company
[2-3 sentences on what specifically you bring that they need]

#### Potential Concerns to Address Proactively
- **Concern 1**: [e.g., "No experience in their specific vertical"]
  - **Counter**: [How your transferable skills apply]
  
- **Concern 2**: [e.g., "They want more years in X"]
  - **Counter**: [Quality of experience vs. years]

#### Strategic Questions to Ask Them
**Product & Role:**
1. [Question about product roadmap/vision]
2. [Question about design team growth plans]
3. [Question about cross-functional collaboration]

**Culture & Process:**
1. [Question about remote work/async communication]
2. [Question about design decision-making authority]
3. [Question about success metrics for this role]

**Business:**
1. [Question about market positioning/competition]
2. [Question about customer feedback loops]

### 8. APPLICATION STRATEGY

#### Emphasis Points
**Lead with:**
- [Specific experience that maps to their needs]
- [Unique positioning - e.g., "first design hire experience"]
- [Understanding of their market/product]

**Demonstrate:**
- Knowledge of their product (use it if possible)
- Understanding of their stage/challenges
- Alignment with values/mission

#### Cover Letter Hook Ideas
1. **Product insight**: "I noticed [X about your product], which reminded me of [your experience]..."
2. **Mission alignment**: "Your approach to [Y] resonates with my experience at [company]..."
3. **Specific value**: "Having scaled design at [company] from X to Y, I see similar opportunities at [their company]..."

#### Interview Prep Priorities
- [ ] Use the product extensively (if accessible)
- [ ] Prepare questions about [specific challenge they likely face]
- [ ] Have examples ready for [type of work they do]
- [ ] Research their competitors' products
- [ ] Connect with current/former employees if possible

## Research Sources to Check

### Primary Sources
- [ ] Company website (About, Careers, Blog)
- [ ] Product (sign up for trial/demo if available)
- [ ] LinkedIn (company page, employee profiles, job posts)
- [ ] Crunchbase/PitchBook (funding, investors)
- [ ] Glassdoor (employee reviews, interview experiences)

### Secondary Sources
- [ ] Tech news (TechCrunch, Product Hunt launches)
- [ ] Industry reports/analysis
- [ ] Competitor analysis (G2, Capterra reviews)
- [ ] Social media (Twitter/X, design community mentions)
- [ ] Podcasts/interviews with founders/leaders

### Design-Specific
- [ ] Dribbble/Behance (team members' work)
- [ ] Design Twitter/LinkedIn (team members' thought leadership)
- [ ] Product screenshots/demos
- [ ] Design job posts (clues about team, tools, process)

---

## Usage Instructions

1. **Provide company name** (+ URL if available)
2. **Add context** about why you're researching (job post, referral, etc.)
3. **Receive comprehensive brief** with strategic recommendations
4. **Use insights** to:
   - Decide whether to apply
   - Craft tailored application
   - Prepare for interviews
   - Ask informed questions

## Output Tone
- Analytical and objective
- Balanced (acknowledge both strengths and concerns)
- Strategic (always connecting back to YOUR positioning)
- Actionable (clear next steps)
- Spanish or English based on user preference

## Notes
- Brief should take 10-15 minutes to read
- Prioritize actionable intel over exhaustive data
- Focus on design-relevant insights
- Flag information gaps that need clarification in interviews
